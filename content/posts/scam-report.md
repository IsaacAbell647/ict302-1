## Why Reporting Matters

When you report a scam, you:
- **Help law enforcement** track and stop scammers
- **Protect others** from becoming victims
- **Create data** that drives security improvements
- **Enable takedowns** of fake websites and accounts
- **Build cases** for prosecution
- **Warn communities** about current threats

**Every report counts!** Even if you didn't lose money, reporting helps prevent future victims.

## Where to Report Scams

### Federal Agencies

#### 1. Federal Trade Commission (FTC)

**Best For:** All types of fraud and scams

**How to Report:**
- **Website:** ReportFraud.ftc.gov
- **Phone:** 1-877-FTC-HELP (1-877-382-4357)
- **Hours:** Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 8 PM ET


#### 2. FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3)

**Best For:** Financial losses over $1,000, serious cybercrimes

**How to Report:**
- **Website:** ic3.gov
- Click "File a Complaint"
- Online form only

**What to Report:**
- Business Email Compromise (BEC)
- Ransomware
- Large financial losses
- Data breaches
- Identity theft
- Investment fraud
- Romance scams with significant loss


#### 3. Department of Justice

**Best For:** Serious fraud, organized crime

**How to Report:**
- U.S. Attorney's Office in your district
- Find yours: justice.gov/usao/find-your-united-states-attorney

### Financial Scams

#### Banks and Credit Cards

**If The Scam Involved Your Account:**

**1. Contact Your Bank Immediately**
- Call fraud department (number on back of card)
- Report fraudulent transactions
- Freeze or cancel compromised cards
- Dispute unauthorized charges

**2. What to Tell Them:**
- Date and time of scam
- How you were contacted
- What information you shared
- Specific transactions to dispute

**3. Follow Up:**
- Get reference number
- Document person you spoke with
- Keep records of all communications
- Monitor account daily

**Important Deadlines:**
- Credit cards: 60 days to report unauthorized charges
- Debit cards: 2 days for full protection, 60 days maximum
- Wire transfers: Contact bank immediately (often irreversible)

#### Credit Bureaus

**If Your Identity Was Stolen:**

**Place Fraud Alert (Free):**
Contact ONE bureau (they notify others):
- **Equifax:** 1-888-766-0008
- **Experian:** 1-888-397-3742
- **TransUnion:** 1-800-680-7289

**Fraud Alert Options:**
- Initial: 1 year, free
- Extended: 7 years, free (requires identity theft report)

**Consider Credit Freeze (Free):**
Contact ALL three bureaus:
- **Equifax:** equifax.com/personal/credit-report-services
- **Experian:** experian.com/freeze
- **TransUnion:** transunion.com/credit-freeze
- **Innovis:** innovis.com (fourth bureau)


#### Investment Fraud

**Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)**
- **Website:** sec.gov/tcr
- **Phone:** 1-800-SEC-0330
- Report investment scams, Ponzi schemes, fraudulent advisors

**Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)**
- **Website:** cftc.gov/complaint
- Report cryptocurrency scams, forex fraud, commodities fraud

**FINRA (Financial Industry Regulatory Authority)**
- **Website:** finra.org/investors/have-problem/file-complaint
- Report problems with brokers and brokerage firms

### Email and Phone Scams

#### Email Phishing

**1. Your Email Provider**
- Gmail: Click three dots → Report phishing
- Outlook: Click  arrow → Phishing → Report
- Yahoo: Click spam → Report phishing

**2. Anti-Phishing Working Group (APWG)**
- **Email:** reportphishing@apwg.org
- Forward suspicious emails
- Include full headers if possible

**3. FTC**
- **Email:** spam@uce.gov
- Forward spam and phishing emails

**4. Impersonated Company**
Forward to their official abuse/security address:
- Amazon: stop-spoofing@amazon.com
- Apple: reportphishing@apple.com
- PayPal: phishing@paypal.com
- IRS: phishing@irs.gov
- Social Security: oig.ssa.gov/report

#### Text Message Scams (Smishing)

**1. Forward to 7726 (SPAM)**
- Free service by all major carriers
- Helps carriers identify and block

**2. Your Carrier**
- **AT&T:** att.com/security → Report fraud
- **Verizon:** verizon.com/security → Report
- **T-Mobile:** reportfraud@t-mobile.com
- **Sprint:** abuse@sprint.com

**3. FTC**
- Screenshot and report at ReportFraud.ftc.gov

#### Phone Scams (Vishing)

**1. Do Not Call Registry**
- **Website:** DoNotCall.gov/report
- **Phone:** 1-888-382-1222
- Report violations of Do Not Call rules

**2. FCC (Federal Communications Commission)**
- **Website:** ConsumerComplaints.fcc.gov
- **Phone:** 1-888-CALL-FCC (1-888-225-5322)
- Report robocalls, caller ID spoofing

**3. Your State Attorney General**
- Find yours: usa.gov/state-attorney-general
- Many have consumer protection divisions

### Social Media Scams

#### Facebook

**How to Report:**
1. Click three dots on post/profile
2. Select "Find Support or Report"
3. Choose appropriate category:
   - Scam or fraud
   - Fake account
   - Impersonation
   - Hacked account

**Report Types:**
- Fake profiles impersonating you
- Scam advertisements
- Marketplace fraud
- Fake friend requests
- Romance scams

**Facebook Specific:**
- facebook.com/help/reportlinks

#### Instagram

**How to Report:**
1. Tap three dots on post/profile
2. Select "Report"
3. Choose:
   - It's spam
   - It's a scam
   - It's pretending to be someone

**For Impersonation:**
- help.instagram.com/contact/636276399721841

#### Twitter/X

**How to Report:**
1. Click three dots on tweet
2. Select "Report Tweet"
3. Choose "It's suspicious or spam"

**For Account Issues:**
- help.twitter.com/forms/spam

#### LinkedIn

**How to Report:**
1. Click three dots on profile/post
2. Select "Report/Block"
3. Choose issue type

**For Fake Profiles:**
- linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/137

#### TikTok

**How to Report:**
1. Long press on video/comment
2. Tap "Report"
3. Select reason

#### WhatsApp

**How to Report:**
1. Open chat
2. Tap contact name
3. Scroll down → Report Contact
4. Block and delete chat

**WhatsApp Specific:**
- WhatsApp → Settings → Help → Contact Us

### Domain/Website Reporting

#### Fake Websites

**1. Web Hosting Provider**
- Find via WHOIS lookup (who.is)
- Contact abuse department
- Provide evidence of fraud

**2. Domain Registrar**
- Report fraudulent domain use
- Request takedown

**3. Google Safe Browsing**
- safebrowsing.google.com/safebrowsing/report_phish
- Helps protect Chrome users

**4. Microsoft SmartScreen**
- microsoft.com/wdsi/support/report-unsafe-site
- Helps protect Edge users

**5. Phishing Databases**
- **PhishTank:** phishtank.com/add_web_phish.php
- **Netcraft:** report.netcraft.com/report

## Employer Reporting (If Work-Related)

### If Scam Involved Work:

**1. IT/Security Team**
- Immediately (within minutes if possible)
- Provide all details
- Follow their instructions
- Don't delete evidence

**2. Your Manager**
- Inform as soon as possible
- Be honest about what happened
- Offer solutions/lessons learned

**3. HR (if applicable)**
- If personal information compromised
- If policy violation occurred

**4. Legal/Compliance (if serious)**
- Data breaches
- Financial losses
- Regulatory implications

**Remember:** Most companies prefer you report immediately rather than hide it.

## Remember


**Key Points:**

1. **Report even if no financial loss**
   - Helps track trends
   - Protects future victims
   - Builds cases

2. **Report quickly**
   - Increases chance of action
   - Better evidence preservation
   - May help recovery

3. **Report multiple places**
   - FTC + IC3 + platform + company
   - Each serves different purpose
   - Maximum impact

4. **Keep records**
   - All evidence
   - All reports made
   - All responses received

5. **Don't feel guilty**
   - Scammers are skilled
   - You're helping by reporting
   - Part of solution, not problem

**Your report makes a difference!** When in doubt, always contact law enforcement. Law enforcement and security teams rely on reports from people like you to identify patterns, shut down operations, and protect others. Thank you for taking the time to report!

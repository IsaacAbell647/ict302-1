Clone Phishing: Deceptive Duplicates

What is Clone Phishing?

Clone phishing is a sophisticated cyber attack where scammers create nearly identical copies of legitimate emails, websites, or social media profiles. Because the message looks familiar and trustworthy, you’re more likely to fall for it.

Types of Clone Phishing

1. Email Cloning

How It Works:

  1. Scammer intercepts or obtains a legitimate email you previously received
  2. They create an exact copy with minor changes
  3. Usually replace legitimate links with malicious ones
  4. Resend from a similar-looking email address
  5. Claim it’s a “resend” or “update” of the original

2. Website Cloning

How It Works: Scammers create perfect copies of legitimate websites.

Email Phishing: The Original Scam

What is Email Phishing?

Email phishing is the most common form of cyber attack, where scammers send fraudulent emails that appear to come from legitimate organizations to trick you into revealing sensitive information, clicking malicious links, or downloading malware. Billions of phishing emails are sent every day worldwide.

Why Email Phishing Remains Effective

Despite decades of awareness, email phishing continues to work because:

  • Volume: Billions sent daily increase chances of success
  • Sophistication: Modern phishing emails look extremely legitimate
  • Human error: We all make mistakes when busy or distracted
  • Evolution: Tactics constantly adapt to security measures
  • Trust: Email still feels more official than other communication
  • Psychological manipulation: Exploits fear, urgency, curiosity

Financial Safety: Protect Your Money Online

Why Financial Safety Matters

Financial scams cost Americans billions annually. Protecting your money online requires awareness and smart habits.

Online Banking Security

Essential Protections

Must Enable:

  • ✓ Two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • ✓ Transaction alerts
  • ✓ Biometric login (fingerprint/face ID)

Best Practices:

  • Use unique, strong passwords
  • Never use public Wi-Fi for banking
  • Use official bank apps only
  • Log out after each session
  • Check account activity weekly

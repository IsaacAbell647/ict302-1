Scam Report

For different tpyes of scams there are different places to report them.

Where to Report

  • Federal Trade Commission (FTC): Click HERE to file a report to help the FTC build cases against scammers.

  • Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3): Click HERE to report scams to the FBI. This helps laws enforcement track trends and investigate crimes.

  • Identity Theft: Click HERE if you are a victim of identity theft, report it to IdentityTheft.gov for a personalized recovery plan.

  • Local Law Enforcement: Contact your local police or the FBI’s local field office to report crimes.

For specific types of scams

  • IRS scams: Report fake IRS, Treasury, or tax-related emails to the IRS by forwarding them as an attachment or providing the email header.

  • Spam text messages: Report spam texts by forwarding them to 7726 (which spells “SPAM”).

  • Financial or investment scams: Contact the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) to report financial scams related to services like credit cards, loans, or debt collection.

Smish

What is a Smishing Attack?

A smishing attack is a from of cyberattack where scammers will use deceptive text messages to trick people into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or Social Security numbers.These attacks often create a sense of urgency or fear and contain malicious links to fake websites or prompts to download malware.

Spear Page

What is a Spear Phishing attack?

A spear phishing attack is a highly targeted cyberattack that sends a fraudulent email or message to a specific person or organization, using personalized information to make it seem credible. The goal is to trick the recipient into revealing sensitive information, such as login credentials or financial details, or downloading malware. These attacks differ from general phishing by their personalized and research-backed approach, which makes them more convincing.

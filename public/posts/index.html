Clone Phishing: Deceptive Duplicates

What is Clone Phishing?

Clone phishing is a sophisticated cyber attack where scammers create nearly identical copies of legitimate emails, websites, or social media profiles. Because the message looks familiar and trustworthy, you’re more likely to fall for it.

Types of Clone Phishing

1. Email Cloning

How It Works:

  1. Scammer intercepts or obtains a legitimate email you previously received
  2. They create an exact copy with minor changes
  3. Usually replace legitimate links with malicious ones
  4. Resend from a similar-looking email address
  5. Claim it’s a “resend” or “update” of the original

2. Website Cloning

How It Works: Scammers create perfect copies of legitimate websites.

Email Phishing: The Original Scam

What is Email Phishing?

Email phishing is the most common form of cyber attack, where scammers send fraudulent emails that appear to come from legitimate organizations to trick you into revealing sensitive information, clicking malicious links, or downloading malware. Billions of phishing emails are sent every day worldwide.

Why Email Phishing Remains Effective

Despite decades of awareness, email phishing continues to work because:

  • Volume: Billions sent daily increase chances of success
  • Sophistication: Modern phishing emails look extremely legitimate
  • Human error: We all make mistakes when busy or distracted
  • Evolution: Tactics constantly adapt to security measures
  • Trust: Email still feels more official than other communication
  • Psychological manipulation: Exploits fear, urgency, curiosity

Financial Safety: Protect Your Money Online

Why Financial Safety Matters

Financial scams cost Americans billions annually. Protecting your money online requires awareness and smart habits.

Online Banking Security

Essential Protections

Must Enable:

  • ✓ Two-factor authentication (2FA)
  • ✓ Transaction alerts
  • ✓ Biometric login (fingerprint/face ID)

Best Practices:

  • Use unique, strong passwords
  • Never use public Wi-Fi for banking
  • Use official bank apps only
  • Log out after each session
  • Check account activity weekly

Card Safety Online

Pharming: The Silent Redirect Attack

What is Pharming?

Pharming redirects you from legitimate websites to fake ones automatically, even when you type the correct URL. Unlike phishing (which tricks you into clicking), pharming manipulates DNS (Domain Name System) to send you to fake sites without your knowledge.

The Danger: You can do everything right, type the URL correctly, use bookmarks, check for HTTPS, and still end up on a fake site.

How It Works

Normal: You type bankofamerica.com → DNS translates to correct IP → You reach real website

Phishing Scams: Don't Take the Bait

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where criminals pretend to be legitimate organizations to trick you into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or Social Security numbers.

Common Types of Phishing

1. Email Phishing

The most common form where attackers send fake emails that appear to come from banks, social media sites, or other trusted sources.

Example Red Flags:

  • Generic greetings like “Dear Customer” instead of your name
  • Urgent language: “Your account will be closed in 24 hours!”
  • Suspicious sender addresses (Check carefully! Scammers can use tricks with characters to make them appear as other characters!)
  • Spelling and grammar errors
  • Requests for personal information

2. Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Smishing: Text Message Scams

What is Smishing?

Smishing (SMS + Phishing) is a cyber attack where scammers send fraudulent text messages to trick you into revealing personal information, clicking malicious links, or sending money. Text messages feel more personal and urgent than emails, making smishing particularly effective.

Why Smishing Works

Psychological Factors:

  • Trust in texts - We assume texts come from real people
  • Immediate urgency - Texts demand quick responses
  • Mobile vulnerability - Harder to verify links on phones
  • Personal device - Phones are with us 24/7
  • Less suspicious - We’re more cautious with emails than texts
  • Direct delivery - No spam folder to filter messages

Spear Phishing: Targeted Personal Attacks

What is Spear Phishing?

Spear phishing is a highly targeted cyber attack where criminals research their victims extensively and craft personalized messages using specific information about you, your job, your relationships, or your activities. Unlike generic phishing that casts a wide net, spear phishing uses a “spear” aimed directly at you.

How Spear Phishing Works

The Attack Process:

1. Research Phase Attackers gather information about you from:

  • Social media profiles (Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, Twitter/X)
  • Company websites (employee directories, press releases)
  • Professional networks
  • Public records

Vishing: Voice Phishing Scams

What is Vishing?

Vishing (voice phishing) is a scam where criminals use phone calls to trick you into revealing personal information, send money, or granting access to your computer or accounts. Vishing relies on direct voice communication to create immediate urgency and pressure.

Why Vishing Works

Scammers use psychological tactics that are designed to be most effective over the phone:

  • Voice creates trust - Hearing a human voice makes the scam feel more legitimate
  • Immediate pressure - You have to respond in real time, without time to think
  • Caller ID spoofing - They can make it look like legitimate organizations are calling
  • Authority intimidation - They impersonate authoritative entities such as the police, IRS, or tech support

Common Vishing Scams

1. IRS/Tax Scams

The Scam: Caller claims you owe back taxes and will be arrested unless you pay immediately.
